An Exhibition of 260 fonts; celebrating uniquely restricted shapes with an almost endless amount of variation.



Opening night, 12th October 7pm—late
Open, 13th & 14th 3—6pm











Level 3, 45 Courtenay Place
Wellington, New Zealand

Located in Wellington’s CBD, this space used to house the Warner Linotype Service Printers back in 1961. We see it to be fitting as this exhibtion is a celebration of typefaces as well as shifting a focus in a gallery towards typography.

Credits

All fonts look the same was curated by Timmy James, Trent Kokich, Matilda Stein, Lili Lovell‑Wood & Giordano Zatta as part of the College of Creative Arts.

Special thank you to Lee Jensen, Fay McAlpine & Tim Turnidge for your guidance and contribution throughout this project.

All typefaces used in the exhibition are credited to the respective foundries and designers. Every effort has been made to contact the designers and foundries who hold the rights to the exhibited typefaces. In a handful of cases, we have had no response to our requests. As this is a nonprofit exhibition run by students, we hope that the rights holders will accept that these typefaces have been exhibited in a spirit of celebration, education and admiration.
